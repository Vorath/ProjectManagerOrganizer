Help

  • To begin, enter in the number of workers as well as the estimated costs for each part of the job (Enter the the highest estimate for the most accurate results)
  • Upon pressing the calculate button, the screen will display the maximum hours per worker that can be spent on any given part of the job
  • Towards the bottom of the screen you will also notice that the total estimated hours that can be spent on the job in order to stay under/on budget as well as the estimated cost are displayed
  • At this point you can begin entering in the number of hours that were actually worked for each part of the job as they are known.
    • When it asks for the "highest hours", this is referring to the highest amount of hours worked by any one worker on that part of the job
      • This information is used to update the suggested amount of hours for the next part of the job
    • When inputting data for the prepack, pack, and unpack, enter the data in terms of how many people did "x" hours of work (Press update and then change your inputs to submit again)
  • As you update the amount of hours that were used for each part of the job, the middle column at the top of the page will display the suggested amount of hours per worker that would need to be done at the maximum in order to get back on budget
  • Once you have pressed the update button for the unpack section, the bottom-most line under the input will display whether you have gone under or over the original estimate, and by how much you have done so
    • Do not worry if you have to input multiple variations of hours for the unpack because when you hit the update button to submit your new data, the amount by which you have gone under or over the original estimate will update as well
